VANILLA SCONES

PREP TIME: 10 MINS | COOK TIME: 14 MINS | TOTAL TIME: 24 MINS
YIELD: 8 SCONES

This is my favorite easy breakfast or afternoon snack with tea, and is sure to impress whoever you share these with. These scones are light, fluffy, and melt in the mouth. This is a great basic recipe if you want to play around with different flavors like lemon, orange, or cinnamon. Even better yet, no need to set out the butter early to soften for those of you like me who are not early risers!

INGREDIENTS

  • ½ CUPS all-purpose flour
  • ½ TEASPOON salt
  • 1 TABLESPOON baking powder
  • ⅓ CUP granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg, any temperature
  • ½ CUP milk
  • 1 TABLESPOON pure vanilla extract (I used vanilla bean paste)
  • ¾ CUP cold unsalted butter, cubed

VANILLA GLAZE

  • 1 ½ CUPS confectioners sugar (also known as powdered sugar)
  • 2 TABLESPOONS water (may need more)
  • 2 TABLESPOONS unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 TABLESPOONS pure vanilla extract (I used vanilla bean paste)

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Preheat oven to 425 DEGREES and line a baking mat with parchment paper or a non-stick baking mat; set aside.
  2. In a stand-alone mixer fitted with a paddle attachment mix together the flour, salt, baking powder, and granulated sugar. Add cold cubed butter and mix on low-speed until course crumbs forms - careful not to over-mix.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the egg, milk, and vanilla then add it to the mixer and mix until dough comes together. It will be crumbly.
  4. Flour a clean service and kneed dough just a bit until it comes together. Form it into a circle about 1 inch in thickness. Use a knife or pizza cutter to cut scones into 8 wedges. Place on baking sheet and sprinkle with granulated sugar. Bake for 10-14 MINS or until edges are slightly golden. Remove from oven and place on a cooling rack to cool.
  5. While scones are cooling, add all the glaze ingredients into a bowl and whisk - glaze will be slightly thick but should drizzle off a spoon. Use a spoon to spread glaze over cooled scones. Let stand until glaze is set.
